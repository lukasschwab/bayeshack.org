Department of Commerce

Prompt #1: Financial Justice

The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) publishes complaints against financial institutions from across the country. This includes ~400K with zipcode level data and information about complaint outcomes (e.g if monetary or non-monetary relief was granted). Many records also include PII-stripped narrative records.

Prompt #2: Nighttime Lights

NOAA collects imagery of the Earth everyday, 24-7. Among the satellites in their fleet, the Suomi NPP Satellite Mission collects imagery of the Earth along a Polar orbit. The data has particular significance for earth scientists, but the the data can be used for far more than atmospheric measures. The Visible Infrared Imaging Radiometer Suite (VIIRS) collects data both during the day and night, enabling an unprecedented set of capabilities to detect adverse events, energy production, and economic activity.

Department of Transportation

Prompt #5: Freight Saftey

Transport of Hazardous Waste. Reducing casualties due to crashes and other safety incidents among all transportation users and minimize risks due to the transportation of hazardous materials. The Federal Highway Administration (FHWA) has developed a Freight Analysis Framework that shows historical freight movement estimates. However, to our knowledge no one has tried to use government permits or related indicators to project future increases in freight traffic.

Department of Human and Health Services

Prompt #6: Provider Accessibility

Since the launch of health insurance marketplaces as part of Obamacare, millions of Americans obtained new health coverage. However, there have been widespread consumer complaints. People often chose the wrong plan, because the relevant information wasn’t available or was inaccurate. For example, consumers would sign up for a plan only to find later that their doctor isn’t in-network or that a specialist they need isn’t taking new patients.

In November 2015, CMS enacted a new regulatory requirement for health insurers who list plans on insurance marketplaces. They must now publish a machine-readable version of their provider network directory, publish it to a specified JSON standard, and update it at least monthly. This data has just started to be openly published in a decentralized manner across many insurers. The challenge is how to create the best consumer app that leverages this newly available data.

Department of Veteran Affairs

Prompt #7: Identifying and Helping Veterans at Risk of Suicide

Suicide among veterans is 50% higher than the national average. Studies have been conducted to understand the clinical and behavioral risk factors behind suicide in the United States. Serious mental health problems are significant contributor to suicidal behavior among veterans, and the public at large. Using openly available data sets (surveys, government reports, etc) can we better understand and predict suicide among veterans as a foundation to provide more targeted and proactive care?
